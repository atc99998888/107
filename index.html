Mission & Vision

Greenfield University's mission is to advance human knowledge and foster global leaders through inquiry-driven education, interdisciplinary research and civic engagement. Our vision is to be a top-50 global university recognised for innovation, inclusion and public impact.

Historical Milestones

  • 1896 — Founded by philanthropist Helen R. Greenfield as a liberal-arts college.
  • 1935 — First PhD degrees awarded in Chemistry and Philosophy.
  • 1964 — Medical Sciences Campus opened; university hospital now serves 320 000 patients annually.
  • 1999 — Center for Environmental Policy launched (ranked #9 worldwide in 2024).
  • 2015 — Achieved carbon-neutral electricity ten years ahead of schedule.

Colleges & Academic Offerings

The University confers 74 bachelor’s, 68 master’s, 24 doctoral and 18 professional certificates across six academic units:

  • College of Arts & Sciences — BA / BSc, MA, MSc, MFA
  • School of Business (AACSB) — BBA, MBA, MS-Finance, PhD-Management
  • School of Engineering (ABET) — BEng, MEng, PhD across eight disciplines
  • School of Law (ABA) — JD, LLM, joint JD/MBA & JD/MPA
  • College of Health Sciences — BS-Nursing, MPH, PharmD, DPT
  • Conservatory of Music & Performing Arts — BMus, MMus, DMA

Research & Innovation

External funding exceeds US $220 million annually. Flagship facilities include:

  • Institute for Advanced Data Science — 200 AI researchers, petaflop HPC cluster
  • Renewable Materials Innovation Lab — Patented carbon-negative cement
  • Center for Precision Medicine — NIH-designated Clinical Research Unit
  • Greenfield Observatory — 4-m optical telescope & radio array

Campus Life & Facilities

• 17 residence halls • 3 eco-friendly dining commons • 22 varsity teams (NCAA Div II)
• 180+ student organisations • 24/7 Health & Wellness Centre • “Green Ride” bike-share

Tuition & Financial Aid

86 % of undergraduates receive assistance. Average first-year aid package 2024-25: US $32 800, combining scholarships, grants, work-study and low-interest loans.

Governance

The University is overseen by a 28-member Board of Trustees chaired by alumna Dr Maria H. Koenig. Daily operations are led by President Prof Samuel D. Whitaker and a 12-member executive cabinet.

Global Partnerships

Exchange agreements with 85 universities in 27 countries—including University of Tokyo, ETH Zurich and University of Cape Town—enable more than 1 100 students each year to study abroad.

Sustainability Commitment

Greenfield aims for net-zero campus operations by 2030. Recent projects cut water use 28 % and landfill waste 64 %, while solar arrays now supply 92 % of electricity.
